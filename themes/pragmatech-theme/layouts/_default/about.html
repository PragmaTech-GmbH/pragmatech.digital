Our Story

PragmaTech was founded with a clear purpose: to help developers build software with confidence.

Our journey began when our founder, Philip, noticed a consistent pattern while consulting for various companies.

Development teams were struggling with the same challenges: unreliable tests, slow feedback cycles, and a lack of confidence when deploying to production.


What started as a collection of blog posts and speaking engagements has evolved into a comprehensive suite of services, courses, and resources designed to address these challenges head-on.


Today, we're proud to have helped hundreds of development teams transform their testing practices and delivery pipelines, enabling them to ship software with confidence.

Our Vision

Our vision is to transform the landscape of software development, where automated testing is not just an afterthought, but a practice integrated into every developer's workflow.

We aspire to create a world where software is consistently reliable, developers are confident in their work, and the process of software delivery is seamless and efficient.

Our Mission

Our mission is to redefine the approach to automated testing in software development by elevating it from an afterthought to a fundamental aspect of the process.

We provide practical, no-nonsense guidance through comprehensive content, hands-on consulting, and targeted workshops that help teams implement effective testing strategies.

Our Core Values

These principles guide everything we do, from the content we create to the way we interact with clients.

Pragmatic Approach

We believe in practical solutions over theoretical perfection. Our recommendations are always grounded in real-world experience and proven patterns.

Continuous Learning

Technology evolves rapidly, and so do we. We're committed to staying at the forefront of best practices and sharing that knowledge openly.

Honest Partnership

We build relationships based on trust and transparency. We're invested in our clients' success and will always recommend what's truly best for their needs.
